3. Correct sales tonnage logic

Sales come from two business flows:

Flow 1:
Quote → Invoice

In this case:

* Invoice updates stock.
* Use invoice lines for tonnage.
* Salesperson comes from invoice.

Flow 2:
Sales Order → Invoice → Delivery

In this case:

* Invoice does not update stock.
* Delivery updates stock.
* Use delivery lines for tonnage.
* Delivery has no salesperson field.
* Salesperson must come from the linked Sales Order using the delivery order number.

Important:

* Do not double-count invoice and delivery for the same sale.
* Count invoice tonnage only where the invoice updates stock.
* Count delivery tonnage where the delivery updates stock.
* For delivery-based sales, enrich salesperson from the linked Sales Order.
* If salesperson cannot be found, keep the row and mark salesperson as Unassigned.
* It is acceptable to exclude analytics/fact rows where item code/ArtCode is missing, because those are not useful for product tonnage analytics.
* Missing ArtCode should be the main reason to skip a sales analytics line.
* Preserve source data as much as practical before transforming it into analytics/fact data.
